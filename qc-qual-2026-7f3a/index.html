Exam format

The exam has a written component and an oral component.

Written (technical memo)

  • Scope: 3–5 papers total: 1 core, 1 context, 1 contrast, up to 2 add-ons.
  • Length: 3–5 pages (excluding references).
  • Include: 1–2 key claims; one worked derivation/example; assumptions/limitations; one concrete extension idea.
  • Verification appendix: brief list of what you personally verified.
  • Submission: Email PDF to Dr. Mantri. Target deadline: February 13, 2026 (unless otherwise arranged).

Oral (presentation + questions)

  • Talk: 30–40 minutes. Focus on one technical thread; avoid a tutorial on minutiae.
  • Questions: ~25 minutes. Questions may range over any paper in your scope and related directions.
  • Ownership check: short whiteboard proof sketch or a concrete example/counterexample.
  • Slides: submit at least 24 hours before the oral exam.

Policies

  • Independence: do the work yourself. Discussion is allowed; collaboration on memo/slides/prepared answers is not.
  • AI tools: allowed for grammar/formatting/slide polishing. If used for technical content, disclose what was used and what you independently verified. You must be able to defend any claim in your memo or slides.

Focused papers

  • Core: the main paper you present in depth; you must defend at least one key technical argument.
  • Context: motivates and positions the core result.
  • Contrast: alternative approach or limitation/negative result clarifying core assumptions.
  • Add-ons: up to two only if used substantively.

Choosing papers: The committee will provide topic packets (each with two anchor papers). Choose one packet, select one anchor as your core, and choose remaining papers to reach 3–5 total. Alternative paper sets require prior committee approval.

Note: Listing many papers is not rewarded; the exam emphasizes technical ownership and live defense.
